Professor Biography

Florence Nightingale (12 May 1820 â€“ 13 August 1910) is an English social reformer and statistician, and the founder of modern nursing. Nightingale came to prominence while serving as a manager and trainer of nurses during the Crimean War, in which she organised care for wounded soldiers.
